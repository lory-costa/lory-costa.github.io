Foundations Core Reflections

02/03/2021

At Dev Academy, we spend a fair amount of time focusing on core learning—soft-skill development. They have us do this because they want to prepare us for the reality of a job in the tech industry—the technical knowledge alone will get us only halfway there.

I thought it was valid exploring the concepts I did, I learned a lot, and they brought a different kind of mental exercise when I needed a break from coding.

What surprised me the most about learning core was that I experienced a refreshing change in recognising my feelings and thoughts. I realised that to be a successful professional I need more than technical knowledge and experience; I need emotional intelligence. I learned that I am confident enough to make mistakes; I rediscovered my love of learning and recognised the importance of knowing my values. It's essential to know who you are and what you feel when trying to figure out where you want to go.

One of the most challenging aspects of core learning was writing my findings. Even though I love writing, it's always testing to put your thoughts into words, but I enjoy the exercise, and I learn a lot from it.

I hope to keep working on my core strengths and learnings and always cherish this experience of exploring them.
