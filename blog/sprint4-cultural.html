Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

17/02/2021

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability the brain has to adapt by forming new neuron connections. It changes how its circuits are wired. When we repeatedly practise an activity or access a memory, we make changes in our brain and our neural networks shape themselves accordingly.

Understanding this concept can positively impact the decisions we make in terms of our commitment to learning. We realise we don't need to be born with a specific set of skills; and that our brains can learn and grow. This knowledge alone is empowering.

Constant practice is critical. Repeating an activity, retrieving a memory, and reviewing materials in various ways helps build more connections in the brain.

Growth Mindset

Studies show that people who understand that their skills can be developed tend to achieve more than those who believe their talents are innate gifts.

A growth mindset views failure as an opportunity for growth rather than evidence of unintelligence. When we think our brains can grow, we behave differently; we worry less about looking smart and put more energy into learning.

On the other hand, the fixed mindset assumes that character, intelligence, and creative ability are static. It dislikes difficult situations and challenges. Striving for success becomes a way of maintaining a sense of being smart or skilled.

We can learn a bit more about what having a growth mindset actually means in Carol Dweck's article on the subject. For a deeper understanding, check her book.
