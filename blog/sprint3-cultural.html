What is Emotional Intelligence?

Also known as emotional quotient or EQ, emotional intelligence is the capacity to recognise and control our actions and emotions—and how they affect people around us. It also means understanding how others feel and responding appropriately.

According to psychologist Daniel Goleman, there are five elements to it:

  • Self-Awareness—understanding our emotions and how our actions affect others.
  • Self-Regulation—monitoring and controlling our behaviour, emotions and thoughts, and adjusting them depending on the situation.
  • Motivation—people with a high degree of EI are usually motivated. They're willing to delay immediate results for long-term success.
  • Empathy—being aware of other people's feelings and emotions and trying to put ourselves in their shoes.
  • Social skills—having the ability to build rapport and trust with others.

    • How is it different from IQ?

    Unlike EI, that encompasses emotions, IQ is more about logic, and it represents abilities such as:

  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Quantitative reasoning

    • Why is Emotional Intelligence important?

    When worked upon, emotional intelligence can help us build stronger personal relationships and successful careers. It can also help us connect with our feelings, be more proactive in searching happiness and making informed decisions about what matters most in life.
