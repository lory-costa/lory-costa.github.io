Problem Solving

19/02/2021

From the moment we are born, we start solving problems. Learning how to talk, hold a spoon, walk, button a shirt are a few examples of the first difficulties humans face. As we grow, some carers help us become better at it. In contrast, others like to solve our problems for us, limiting our development in this area. In either case, there comes a season in life when we have to figure things out independently.

For various reasons, different people have different approaches to problem-solving. Still, it's a valuable skill, and we should all practice it. I am a naturally analytical person, so I have a system to deal with challenges. First, I need to understand what the problem is. Second, I explore possible strategies and choose one; only then will I solve the problem. The last and perhaps more important part of the process is to look back and reflect on the solution.

In the past few weeks, I have learned a few new problem-solving techniques that will help me become a better web developer. Among my favourites are pseudocode, try and error, console.logging, googling, and, of course, improving my process with reflection. I know I still have a long way to go, but I feel confident I can tackle any problem with some persistence and a fair amount of optimism.

When we think about it, what fun would life be without challenges? How would we grow, improve, achieve our goals, live life to its fullest if we didn't have problems to teach us? From this perspective, I am thankful for the obstacles in my way and for having these new skills to help me deal with them.
